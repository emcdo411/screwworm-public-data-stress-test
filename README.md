## Executive Brief

This repository contains a public-signal enterprise risk stress test for the **New World Screwworm (NWS)** threat environment affecting the cattle industry, South Texas, and a King Ranch-style enterprise.

The model does not claim private King Ranch financial data.

It translates public signals into executive decision intelligence across:

- Cattle economics
- Border closure and supply constriction
- Operational readiness
- Wildlife-livestock interface risk
- Hunting and recreation exposure
- Retail and heritage-brand trust
- USDA / TAHC response dependency
- Decision timing

The core question:

> What would leadership do differently Monday morning if this model is directionally correct?

## Core Finding

The stress test produced a **Conditional Pass**.

The original model survives directionally, but exact dollar ranges should be treated as **scenario bands**, not point forecasts.

The decision logic still holds:

> Readiness is not a precautionary expense. It is a low-cost option against a high-cost surprise event.

## The Decision Line

Preparedness converts unbounded surprise cost into bounded operational exposure.

The stress test estimates that a readiness investment of approximately **$350K-$750K** can materially reduce exposure to an unprepared enterprise-risk event estimated in the **$12M-$28M** directional range.

This does not require certainty.

It requires asymmetry math.

## What The Stress Test Changed

The first model identified a clear readiness case.

The stress test sharpened it by identifying where the original assumptions could break:

1. **Price upside may not flow cleanly to the operator.**  
   Sales timing, regional basis, feedlot demand, futures volatility, drought, and movement rules can reduce or reverse the benefit.

2. **Labor and inspection cadence are likely underestimated.**  
   The cost is not just treatment. It is repeated wound surveillance across cattle, wildlife zones, working animals, and contractor contact points.

3. **Wildlife exposure may be larger than the base model assumes.**  
   For large-acreage South Texas operations, a 1.6x-2.4x multiplier may be more realistic in severe cases.

4. **Brand exposure is a real enterprise variable.**  
   A biological event becomes a brand-confidence event when the public can no longer separate the health risk from the name on the gate.

5. **Public-sector response is necessary but not sufficient.**  
   USDA/APHIS and TAHC response matter, but ranch-level readiness cannot depend entirely on external timing.

## Brand And Retail Exposure

For retail and brand leadership, the important question is not whether the retail division manages cattle-health response.

The important question is:

> When does an animal-health event become a customer-confidence event?

The risk pathway:

```text
Regional detection
    -> media attention
    -> customer questions
    -> hunting guest anxiety
    -> tourism hesitation
    -> retail trust pressure
    -> brand-story management
```

Retail leadership should know before the public asks:

- What has been detected
- Where it was detected
- Whether the enterprise has a readiness protocol
- Whether customer-facing language is already approved
- Whether Saddle Shop, hunting, tourism, and brand teams have a shared narrative

The retail risk is not infection.

The retail risk is uncertainty plus silence.

## Public-Sector Dependency Read

The public-sector dependency risk is not whether USDA understands the threat.

USDA/APHIS has stated that New World Screwworm is not currently present in the United States, southern livestock ports are closed to livestock trade, and sterile fly dispersal is active in Mexico and along the border.

The bigger dependency is field-level timing:

- Trap coverage
- Reporting latency
- Sterile fly availability
- Treatment authorization
- Cross-border coordination
- County-level response capacity
- State/federal communication speed

What a King Ranch-style enterprise should control internally regardless of public-sector performance:

- Wound surveillance SOP
- Crew training
- Animal movement controls
- Contractor and visitor animal policy
- Veterinary escalation path
- Wildlife observation protocol
- Communications trigger map
- Executive decision cadence

Can the decision-maker answer in one sentence what the economics says they should do differently?

**Yes.**

> Fund and activate a minimum viable screwworm readiness cell now because the cost of readiness is bounded and controllable, while the cost of surprise is variable, brand-compounding, and operationally asymmetric.

## Bottom Line

The model does not require certainty to be useful.

It requires leadership to recognize asymmetry.

If readiness costs hundreds of thousands and surprise can cost tens of millions, the decision is not whether the outbreak is guaranteed.

The decision is whether the organization wants to meet the event prepared, or let the event define the organization first.
