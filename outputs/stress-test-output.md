MBEL STRESS TEST VERDICT  
**CONDITIONAL PASS**

The original model survives the stress test directionally, but its strongest claim needs a tighter caveat:

Preparedness is economically rational, but the exact dollar spread between “prepared” and “unprepared” is highly sensitive to three variables: actual affected-head count, wildlife interface detection lag, and public-sector containment speed.

The decision case still holds. The dollar ranges should be treated as **scenario bands**, not point forecasts.

---

## 1. ORIGINAL MODEL CLAIM

The original model argues that New World Screwworm is already an economic event because southern livestock ports are closed, feeder cattle supply is constrained, and cow-calf operators may see price upside before any U.S. detection occurs. The larger enterprise-risk claim is that if NWS enters South Texas, a King Ranch-style operation faces a non-linear cost surge through labor, veterinary response, movement restriction, wildlife interface, hunting/recreation confidence, and brand exposure. The core decision claim is that a **$350K-$750K readiness investment** can convert an unprepared **$14M-$22M enterprise-risk event** into a prepared **$2M-$5M operational-loss event**.

---

## 2. ASSUMPTIONS MOST LIKELY TO BREAK

1. **Price upside flows cleanly to the operator**  
   This is the most fragile assumption. Border closure supports domestic supply tightening, but realized benefit depends on sales timing, local basis, cattle class, feedlot demand, drought, and movement rules.

2. **Affected-head count remains bounded**  
   The model assumes detection does not cascade widely across the herd. If wound surveillance is late, the cost curve steepens quickly.

3. **Wildlife multiplier is only 1.3x-2.0x**  
   For a large South Texas operation with deer, feral hogs, working horses, dogs, hunting operations, and wide acreage, the multiplier may need to be **1.6x-2.4x** in severe cases.

4. **USDA/TAHC response is fast enough to contain spread**  
   USDA says NWS is not present in the U.S., all southern livestock ports are closed, and sterile fly dispersal is active in Mexico and along the border. But operational protection still depends on reporting speed, trap coverage, sterile fly availability, and field execution.

5. **Brand exposure remains secondary**  
   This could break if media framing shifts from “animal health risk” to “South Texas ranch outbreak.” For heritage brands, public trust can move faster than operational facts.

## 5. BRAND / RETAIL EXPOSURE READ

For a retail or brand executive, the key point is this:

An animal-health event becomes a brand-confidence event when the public can no longer separate the biological risk from the name on the gate.

For King Ranch-style heritage brands, the risk pathway is:

**confirmed regional detection → media attention → consumer questions → hunting guest anxiety → tourism hesitation → retail trust pressure → brand story management**

Retail leadership does not need to manage cattle operations. But retail leadership should know:

- What has been detected
- Where it was detected
- Whether the ranch has a readiness protocol
- Whether the public message is already written
- Whether customer-facing teams know what to say
- Whether the Saddle Shop, hunting, tourism, and brand teams have a shared narrative

**Communications trigger to pre-position:**  
If NWS is detected in South Texas or within a defined county/radius threshold, King Ranch should have a plain-language assurance message ready that says the enterprise is monitoring, coordinating with animal-health authorities, protecting livestock and wildlife, and maintaining customer-facing operations responsibly.

The retail risk is not infection. The retail risk is uncertainty plus silence.

---

## 6. PUBLIC-SECTOR DEPENDENCY READ

The biggest public-sector dependency risk is not whether USDA understands the threat. USDA clearly does. APHIS states that NWS is not currently present in the U.S., southern livestock ports are closed, and sterile insects are being dispersed in Mexico and along the U.S.-Mexico border.

The biggest risk is **field-level timing**:

- How quickly detections are reported
- Whether trap coverage catches movement early
- Whether sterile fly dispersal is sufficient in the right locations
- Whether treatment access is smooth
- Whether state/federal communication reaches ranch operators fast enough
- Whether county-level response capacity is adequate

What King Ranch should control internally regardless of USDA performance:

- Wound surveillance SOP
- Crew training
- Animal movement controls
- Contractor/visitor animal policy
- Veterinary escalation path
- Wildlife observation protocol
- Communications trigger map
- Executive decision cadence
- Documentation standard for suspected cases

Public-sector containment is necessary. It is not a substitute for private operational readiness.

## 7. READINESS CELL ECONOMIC CASE

**Base readiness investment:** $350K-$750K  
**Revised expected avoided-loss range:** $4M-$18M depending on scenario path  
**Base asymmetry ratio:** roughly **6x-24x** avoided exposure per readiness dollar  
**Severe asymmetry ratio:** can exceed **30x** if readiness prevents late detection and brand escalation

Simple expected-value frame:

- Scenario C unprepared loss midpoint: **-$20M**
- Scenario B prepared loss midpoint: **-$5M**
- Avoided downside: **$15M**
- Readiness cost midpoint: **$550K**
- Gross asymmetry: **~27x**

Even if the model is cut in half:

- Avoided downside: **$7.5M**
- Readiness cost: **$750K**
- Asymmetry: **10x**

**Break-even threshold:**  
If readiness reduces the probability or severity of one major detection failure by even **3%-5%**, the investment is economically defensible.

**Decision recommendation:**  
Proceed with a minimum viable readiness cell now, not a bloated program. The goal is not bureaucracy. The goal is early detection, disciplined escalation, and message control.

Minimum viable readiness cell:

- Cattle operations lead
- Wildlife lead
- Veterinary lead
- Communications/brand lead
- Retail/customer-facing liaison
- Legal/compliance contact
- Weekly NWS status review while border risk remains elevated
