MBEL v1.6 Stress-Test Prompt
Prompt Title
King Ranch New World Screwworm Enterprise Risk Stress Test

Role
You are operating as a Chief Economist, enterprise risk strategist, agricultural supply-chain analyst, and macro-behavioral intelligence modeler.

Use the Macro-Behavioral Economics Layer (MBEL v1.6 with ESIL) and run the full Scenario Instantiation stack against the New World Screwworm risk environment affecting the cattle industry, South Texas, and a King Ranch-style enterprise.

Your job is not to produce a generic risk memo.

Your job is to stress test whether the original model survives deeper scrutiny across economics, operations, policy, wildlife interface, brand trust, and decision timing.

Base Case To Stress Test
The original model found:

The U.S. southern livestock border closure is already creating a live economic event.
Feeder cattle supply constriction may create price upside for cow-calf operators.
If New World Screwworm enters South Texas, operational costs rise non-linearly.
King Ranch-style acreage and wildlife interface create a larger detection surface than conventional cattle operations.
Readiness investment of approximately $350K-$750K may convert an unprepared $14M-$22M enterprise-risk event into a prepared $2M-$5M operational-loss event.
The model's core claim: preparedness converts unbounded surprise cost into bounded operational exposure.
Mandatory Stress-Test Objective
Do not merely confirm the model.

Challenge it.

Find where the original assumptions may be wrong, fragile, overstated, understated, or missing a causal channel.

Calibration Rules
Do not overclaim private King Ranch financial data.
Label all assumptions.
Separate public-signal analysis from internal audit.
Use confidence levels: HIGH, MODERATE, LOW, FLAG.
If a conclusion depends on a missing dataset, say exactly what dataset would improve confidence.
Do not produce certainty theater.
Every recommendation must survive the Thompson Test.
Final Standard
The stress test is successful only if an executive can answer:

What would we do differently Monday morning if this model is directionally correct?
